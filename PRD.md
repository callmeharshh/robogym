## 1. TL;DR

Robots can't learn to manipulate the physical world without huge amounts of human demonstration data — and that data barely exists. Leading labs collect it by paying teleoperators to puppet robots one task at a time: slow, expensive, and narrow. **RoboGym turns collecting it into a game.** Players steer a simulated robot arm through manipulation puzzles (reach, sort, stack); every session is silently recorded as a labeled, LeRobot-compatible training episode and pooled into an open dataset. After you demonstrate, the game trains a policy and **the arm plays on its own** — closing the full human-to-autonomous loop in a browser.

The game is the **viral front door**. The pooled, high-diversity dataset is the **fundable moat**.

## 2. Vision & mission

- **Mission:** Build the open, crowd-sourced data layer that helps robots learn to act in the real world.
- **Vision:** The largest and most *diverse* open demonstration dataset in robotics — generated by millions of players, owned by the commons, and used by every lab and indie researcher on the LeRobot / Hugging Face ecosystem.
- **Why us / why now:** Capital is flooding Physical AI (~$27.6B in 2025), but it flows to (a) labs hoarding in-house data and (b) teleop-farm vendors selling it at warehouse cost. Nobody is building the data layer *the open way — by the crowd, for the crowd*. A fast-moving solo builder with consumer-product instincts can own that wedge before the labs (who lack that instinct) do.

## 3. The problem

AI learned to see and write from the internet's data. But **there is no internet of physical actions** — no equivalent corpus of "how a hand/gripper moves to accomplish a task." So robots still can't reliably pick up a cup.

**Evidence the bottleneck is real:**
- Physical Intelligence (π₀, 2024): *"There is no such treasure trove of robot data… large amounts of data need to be collected with that particular robot, for that particular application."* (Lab now valued ~$5.6B.)
- Skild AI (2025): *"One of the biggest challenges in building a robotics foundation model is the lack of any large-scale robotics data."*
- Generalist AI's GEN-0 (Nov 2025) showed robot data follows **power-law scaling laws** — more (and more diverse) data reliably improves policies.

**The nuance (important — this shapes our positioning):** The field has shifted from *"we need more data"* to *"we need the* ***right*** *data"* — **diversity, action-labels, and physics fidelity** over raw volume. Diversity of strategies/scenes is the axis labs cannot simply buy. That is precisely what a game with thousands of players produces.

## 4. The product — how it works

### 4.1 Player experience
1. **Land** on the marketing site → one click to play, no signup.
2. **Onboard:** pick a trainer handle, choose a mode (Time Attack 60s / Zen).
3. **Play:** move the mouse to steer a 2-link robot arm; hold click / Space to close the gripper. Solve rotating tasks:
   - **REACH** — grab a block, drop it on the target zone.
   - **SORT** — drop a colored block in the matching colored bin.
   - **STACK** — balance a block on top of a base block.
4. **Get rewarded:** score, combo multipliers (up to ×8), particle/sound feedback, time bonuses, a rank that levels up (Trainee → Operator → Pilot → Specialist → Lead → Director), and a leaderboard.
5. **See the point:** a live "demos contributed / frames recorded" counter, a **Dataset inspector** showing the real JSON being generated, an **Export** button, a shareable **PNG score card**, and **"Watch the AI play what you taught it"** (see 4.3).

### 4.2 What's happening under the hood (the actual value)
Every ~50ms (~20 Hz) while playing, the game records one frame of a **state → action trajectory**:
- **Observation:** joint angles `[θ₁, θ₂]`, end-effector position `[x, y]`, object position `[x, y]`, **goal position `[x, y]`**, gripper state `{0,1}`.
- **Action:** target end-effector `[x, y]`, gripper command `{0,1}`.

Frames are grouped into **episodes** (one per task attempt, labeled success/failure). On export, they're bundled into a **LeRobot-like dataset** — the same shape real robot policies train on.

### 4.3 The loop closes — autonomous play (autopilot)
After a player demonstrates, RoboGym trains an **imitation-learning policy** from those demonstrations and the arm can **play the game on its own**. This is the entire thesis of Physical AI, made watchable in a browser: *human demonstrates → policy learns → robot acts autonomously → more demonstrations make it better.*

- **Method:** a nearest-neighbour behavioural-cloning policy. Every demonstrated `(situation → action)` pair is stored; when driving, the policy finds the closest situations the crowd has been in and copies those actions.
- **Generalisation:** features are translation-invariant (`ee→object`, `object→goal`) and scale-invariant (normalised by arm reach), so a policy learned in one layout transfers to new ones.
- **The flywheel, visible:** the policy improves as more is played — and because it learns from the *pooled* data, everyone's play makes everyone's robot better.
- **Data integrity:** the AI's own autonomous play is **never** recorded as human demonstration data (that would be circular and poison the dataset).

## 5. Positioning & differentiation

**The pitch:** *"Foldit for robot manipulation."* Position as **the diversity layer for the LeRobot ecosystem** — NOT a teleop-farm replacement (XDOF raised $70M to do that; we can't and shouldn't try to out-capital them).

**Be radically honest (this earns credibility instead of getting dunked on):** RoboGym data is **co-training + demo-amplification input, not standalone real-robot training data.** A 2D screen game can't capture force/contact/depth, and sim-only policies transfer poorly on their own. But:
- **Diversity beats volume** past a threshold (Data Scaling Laws, ICLR 2025).
- **A few demos amplify massively** (MimicGen turned ~200 human demos into 50,000+).
- **Sim + a little real data co-training** boosts real-world success meaningfully.

**White space (validated by research):** *No public browser game currently exports LeRobot-format data.* The closest precedents prove the pieces work but none combine them:
- **RoboCade** (Stanford, Dec 2025) — gamified collection lifted policy success **16–56%**, but uses a physical controller, lab-only, no LeRobot output. **← our credibility benchmark to beat.**
- **RoboCrowd** (Berkeley) — gamified crowdsourcing on real ALOHA hardware, in person.
- **RoboTurk** (Stanford) — phone teleop, framed as paid work, not a game.
- **Mecka** — funded, gamified *mobile app* but collects video, not trajectories.

**Target embodiment:** the **SO-101 arm**, which dominates (>50%) the LeRobot Hub — so our data maps onto what the open community actually uses.

## 7. Technical architecture

### 7.1 Tech stack
| Layer | Choice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Frontend** | Vanilla HTML + CSS + JavaScript | Zero build step, zero dependencies, $0, deploy-anywhere. |
| **Rendering** | HTML5 Canvas 2D | Lightweight real-time arm/physics/particles. |
| **Audio** | Web Audio API (procedural) | No asset files; tiny; works offline. |
| **Autonomy** | In-browser nearest-neighbour imitation policy (no ML libs) | Closes the human→autonomous loop; learns + runs live in the browser. |
| **Type** | Google Fonts (Bricolage Grotesque, Hanken Grotesk, JetBrains Mono) | Premium identity. |
| **Local persistence** | `localStorage` | Handle, totals, leaderboard, learned policy — works with no backend. |
| **Backend** *(planned)* | **Supabase** (Postgres + auto REST API + Row Level Security), free tier | Global counter, leaderboard, pooled dataset — no server to run. |
| **Hosting** | **Netlify** (Git-connected, auto-deploy) | Free, instant public URL, custom domain support. |
| **Data format** | LeRobot-like JSON episodes | De-facto open standard; drops into Hugging Face pipelines. |

> **Design principle:** stay dependency-free and static for as long as possible. Every feature degrades gracefully to local-only if the backend is absent.

### 7.3 Game engine internals
- **Inverse kinematics:** a 2-link planar arm solves joint angles so the end-effector reaches toward a target (law-of-cosines, clamped to reach). Drives both human (cursor) and autopilot (policy) control.
- **Physics:** simple gravity + floor collision per object; held objects track the end-effector; stack task adds a "rest on top of base" floor check.
- **Tasks:** a small task system spawns objects/bins/targets and defines a success predicate; early play favors REACH, then all three rotate with scaling difficulty.
- **Scoring:** `score += 100 × multiplier`; multiplier grows with consecutive solves within a 7s window; Time Attack adds +2s per solve.
- **Logging:** throttled to ~20 Hz; frames accumulate into the current episode; success flushes the episode into the export buffer and teaches the policy live.
- **Autopilot:** at ~18 Hz the policy reads the current situation, predicts a smoothed target for the IK solver + a gripper command, and drives the arm; logging is disabled while autonomous.

### 7.4 Data flow
```
play → 20Hz frame capture → episode buffer ─┬→ JSON export (LeRobot-like)
                                            ├→ teach imitation policy (live)
                          endRun → cloud.submit(score) ─┐
                          endRun → cloud.contribute(data)┴→ Supabase (global pool + leaderboard)
boot → cloud.refresh() → global_totals() + top scores → UI
autopilot → policy.predict(situation) → IK → arm acts on its own (not recorded)
```

## 8. Data model

### 8.1 Exported dataset (LeRobot-like)
```json
{
  "meta": {
    "env": "robogym-2d-v0",
    "format": "lerobot-like",
    "fps": 20,
    "action_space": "target_ee[x,y] + gripper{0,1}",
    "observation_space": "joint_angles[2], ee[2], object[2], goal[2], gripper{0,1}",
    "contributor": "neo_arm",
    "episodes": 12,
    "total_frames": 1840
  },
  "episodes": [
    {
      "episode_index": 0,
      "task": "stack",
      "success": true,
      "frames": [
        {
          "t": 2.40,
          "observation": { "joint_angles": [-0.94, 1.21], "ee": [412, 268], "object": [395, 301], "goal": [430, 300], "gripper": 1 },
          "action": { "target_ee": [410, 260], "gripper": 1 }
        }
      ]
    }
  ]
}
```

### 8.2 Backend schema (Supabase / Postgres)
- **`scores`** — `id, created_at, handle, score, demos, combo, mode` → powers the global leaderboard and the global demo count (`sum(demos)`).
- **`contributions`** — `id, created_at, handle, demos, frames, data (jsonb)` → the accumulating training-data asset (the moat).
- **`global_totals()`** — RPC returning `{ demos, runs }` for the live counters.
- **Security:** Row Level Security on; anon role may INSERT scores/contributions and SELECT scores only (no deletes). Full SQL is in `GO_LIVE.md`.

### Phase 3 — Prove the moat
- Run a **downstream-lift experiment:** train/fine-tune a policy with vs. without RoboGym data; measure success-rate delta. **Goal: beat RoboCade's 16–56%.** A published number here is the single most fundable artifact we can produce.
- Demo-amplification pipeline (MimicGen-style) to multiply collected demos.
- Data quality tooling: filter noisy/low-effort sessions, weight by diversity.

### Phase 4 — Business
- **Monetization:** paid dataset access / API + sponsored collection campaigns (a lab sponsors a specific task; players farm it). Pipelines/tooling for the 16,000+ LeRobot projects.
- Contributor incentives (revenue share / bounties) to sustain the data flywheel.

## 12. Risks & honest mitigations

| Risk | Reality | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **2D / sim ≠ real robots** | Screens lose force, contact, depth; sim-only policies can drop to ~10% real success. | Position as *co-training + amplification* data, never standalone. Move to 3D (Phase 2). Target a real embodiment (SO-101). |
| **Crowd data is noisy** | Variable player skill/speed can "poison" learning. | Success labels, per-session quality filtering, diversity weighting. AI play is never recorded. |
| **"It's just a toy"** | Skeptics will say a game can't train robots. | Lead with the honest framing + a published downstream-lift number (Phase 3). The live autopilot makes the learning *tangible*. |
| **Bottleneck thesis is contested** | Some argue it's deployment/eval, not data. | We sell *diversity* specifically — the sub-claim with the strongest empirical support. |
| **Solo founder bandwidth** | One person, big scope. | Stay dependency-free; ship narrow; let the community/data flywheel do the heavy lifting. |

## 15. Glossary

- **Physical AI / Embodied AI** — AI that perceives and acts in the physical world (robots, manipulation, locomotion).
- **Manipulation data** — recordings of how an arm/gripper accomplishes physical tasks.
- **Demonstration / episode** — one recorded attempt at a task (a trajectory of states + actions).
- **Imitation learning / behavioural cloning** — training a policy to reproduce demonstrated actions for a given situation. RoboGym's autopilot is an instance of this.
- **Nearest-neighbour (kNN) policy** — a non-parametric policy that acts by finding the most similar past situations and copying their actions. Needs no training step — ideal for the browser.
- **Policy** — the function mapping an observation (situation) to an action; "the robot's brain."
- **LeRobot** — Hugging Face's open robotics framework + dataset format; the de-facto open standard.
- **Teleoperation** — a human remotely puppeting a robot; the dominant (expensive) way data is collected today.
- **Co-training** — training on a mix of data sources (e.g., sim + real) so cheaper data boosts a model alongside scarce real data.
- **Demo-amplification** — turning a few human demos into many synthetic ones (e.g., MimicGen).
- **IK (inverse kinematics)** — solving joint angles to place the arm's tip at a target point.
- **SO-101** — a popular low-cost robot arm that dominates the LeRobot dataset hub.
